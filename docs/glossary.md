**Accountability** — An obligation to explain, justify, answer for, correct or remedy an action, decision or failure.

**Actor** — A governed entity capable of requesting, approving, denying, performing, supervising, recording or being affected by an action.

**Agent** — A software actor capable of selecting or executing actions based on instructions, goals, context, policy, evidence or observed state.

**Assertion** — A claim made by an identified actor or artifact.

**Assurance** — A reasoned, evidence-based conclusion regarding whether specified requirements or properties are satisfied within a defined scope and time.

**Authority** — A recognised and bounded ability to permit, perform, approve, prohibit, delegate or constrain an effect.

**Capability** — A technical or operational ability to perform an action or produce an effect.

**Constraint** — A limitation on how, when, where or under what conditions an action may occur.

**Decision receipt** — A governance record describing why a proposed effect was permitted, denied, restricted, suspended or routed for review.

**Delegation** — A bounded grant of authority from a delegator to a delegate.

**Effect** — A material change in rights, obligations, resources, access, state, risk or opportunity.

**Evidence** — Information or an artifact used to support or challenge a claim, requirement, verification, assessment or decision.

**Governance context** — The institutional, legal, contractual, jurisdictional, technical and operational environment in which a governance decision is interpreted.

**Governance event** — An attributable occurrence that materially changes authority, policy, assurance, status, eligibility, accountability or remedy state.

**Obligation** — A required action triggered by a condition, event or prior action.

**Permission** — An authorisation to perform an action or admit an effect.

**Policy** — A human-auditable normative requirement or coherent set of requirements.

**Principal** — An actor whose interests, rights, resources or authority are represented or exercised by another actor.

**Prohibition** — A requirement that forbids an action or effect.

**Recognition** — A governed decision to accept another actor’s authority, status, assurance or determination for a defined purpose.

**Registry** — A governed service or record system that publishes or resolves assertions, status or references about defined subjects or relationships.

**Role** — A context-specific capacity in which an actor participates.

**Rule** — A machine-testable expression of a requirement.

**Trust decision** — A contextual outcome determining whether and under what constraints a relying party is prepared to permit, deny, restrict, suspend or review a proposed effect.

**Trust graph** — A governed graph of typed, attributable and contextual relationships used as evidence in trust decisions.

**Verification** — A process that checks whether a specified condition or assertion holds.
