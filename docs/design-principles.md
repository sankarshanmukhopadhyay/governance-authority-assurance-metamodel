1. **Authority before action.** Consequential effects require explicit authority evaluation.
2. **Context before trust.** Reliance conclusions depend on purpose, policy, risk and time.
3. **Evidence before assurance.** Assurance conclusions must identify supporting evidence and limitations.
4. **Accountability survives delegation.** Delegation reallocates authority but does not erase responsibility.
5. **Governance operates at runtime.** Static approval is insufficient where material conditions change during execution.
6. **Relationships are typed.** Graph edges, recognitions and delegations require explicit semantics.
7. **Failure is explicit.** Missing evidence, stale status or unresolved authority must not silently become permission.
8. **Affected parties remain visible.** Governance must address people and organisations experiencing consequences, including those outside the operating consortium.
9. **Machine actionability remains traceable to human authority.** Executable rules must map to an authorised normative source.
10. **Interoperability does not imply recognition.** Technical exchange and governance acceptance are distinct decisions.
