# Glossary

{% include gaam-meta.html %}

## Terminology alignment

GAAM aligns shared terminology with the **CTWG Main Glossary v1.4.1** where the CTWG definition is compatible with GAAM's scope. GAAM retains explicit local extensions where governance execution requires stronger treatment of authority, delegation, enforcement, revocation, evidence or remedy. The normative meaning of a term in GAAM is the definition on this page; the alignment crosswalk records whether that meaning is adopted, extended or GAAM-specific.

See the [CTWG glossary alignment crosswalk](../mappings/ctwg-v1.4.1-glossary-alignment.md).

**Accountability** — An obligation to explain, justify, answer for, correct or remedy an action, decision or failure.

**Actor** — An entity that can act or execute actions. In GAAM, an actor may be a person, machine or software component; an organization participates through one or more authorized actors.

**Agent** — An actor that executes an action on behalf of a principal. In GAAM, an agent may be software-based and may select or execute actions using instructions, goals, context, policy, evidence or observed state.

**Assertion** — A claim made by an identified actor or artifact.

**Assurance** — A reasoned, evidence-based conclusion regarding whether specified requirements or properties are satisfied within a defined scope and time.

**Authority** — A recognised and bounded ability to permit, perform, approve, prohibit, delegate or constrain an effect.

**Capability** — The ability or permission for an actor or agent to perform a specific action on behalf of a party within a defined scope and subject to applicable constraints.

**Constraint** — A limitation on how, when, where or under what conditions an action may occur.

**Decision receipt** — A structured, integrity-protected governance record describing a trust or governance decision, including its context, authority, policy, evidence, outcome, obligations and traceability data.

**Delegation** — The act of a delegator authorizing a delegatee to perform a defined set of actions on the delegator’s behalf within an authorized scope and subject to applicable constraints.

**Effect** — A material change in rights, obligations, resources, access, state, risk or opportunity.

**Evidence** — Information or an artifact used to support or challenge a claim, requirement, verification, assessment or decision.

**Governance context** — The institutional, legal, contractual, jurisdictional, technical and operational environment in which a governance decision is interpreted.

**Governance event** — An attributable occurrence that materially changes authority, policy, assurance, status, eligibility, accountability or remedy state.

**Obligation** — A required action triggered by a condition, event or prior action.

**Permission** — An authorization to perform an action or admit an effect within a defined scope.

**Policy** — Statements, rules or assertions that specify required, permitted, prohibited or expected behavior within a defined scope. A policy may be human-readable, machine-readable or both, and may be interpreted, enforced or audited by people, software or both.

**Principal** — The party for whom, or on behalf of whom, an actor executes an action.

**Prohibition** — A requirement that forbids an action or effect.

**Recognition** — A governed decision to accept another actor’s authority, status, assurance or determination for a defined purpose.

**Registry** — A governed record system that serves as an authoritative source of information about defined entities, subjects, assertions, status or relationships.

**Role** — A named, context-specific set of responsibilities, expected behaviors, permitted actions or required knowledge associated with an entity.

**Rule** — A prescribed, machine-testable expression of a requirement, conduct, process or action intended to achieve a defined result or objective.

**Trust decision** — A contextual decision by a party about whether, and under what constraints, to engage in an interaction or permit a proposed effect that involves real or perceived risk.

**Trust graph** — A data structure describing typed, attributable and contextual trust relationships among two or more entities. It may support transitive reasoning, but GAAM requires every resulting decision to remain bounded by policy, evidence, scope and assurance.

**Verification** — An action performed to determine whether a claim, assertion, condition or data object is authentic or otherwise satisfies specified criteria.
