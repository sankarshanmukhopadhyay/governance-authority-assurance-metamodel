# Example: Runtime Governance Evaluation

Before a service admits a consequential effect, it evaluates:

1. actor and agent identifiers;
2. authority source and delegation lineage;
3. policy and rule versions;
4. evidence provenance and freshness;
5. assurance state;
6. boundary crossings;
7. revocation and suspension state;
8. risk thresholds and human-review triggers.

The outcome is one of permit, deny, restrict, suspend or review. A proportionate decision receipt preserves the evaluated governance state without disclosing unnecessary confidential evidence.
