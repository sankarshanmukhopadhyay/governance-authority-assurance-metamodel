# Frequently Asked Questions

## Is GAAM an identity specification?

No. Identity may support attribution, but GAAM focuses on authority, governance, evidence, decisions and accountability.

## Is GAAM an AI governance framework?

No. It includes agentic-system requirements because agents create authority and accountability challenges, but the metamodel also applies to human-led and conventional distributed systems.

## Does technical interoperability imply governance recognition?

No. Systems may exchange data while refusing to recognise one another’s authority, assurance or decisions.

## Does a credential prove authority?

Only when its semantics, issuer authority, subject binding, scope, status and applicable policy establish the relevant authority. Possession alone is insufficient.

## Is a trust graph a scoring system?

Not necessarily. GAAM treats trust graphs as governed sets of typed assertions and relationships. A relying party still makes a contextual decision.

## Must implementations use decentralised technology?

No. The metamodel is architecture-neutral and applies to centralised, federated, decentralised and hybrid systems.
